The musical composition "Van Der Graaf Generator" is an Indie track recorded in 2002
. The song features music adapted by Serhiy Zarudnyak from a vocal melody of a Bulgarian folk song performed by Penka Pavlova
. The lyrics are an original work by Serhiy Zarudnyak, and the recording and arrangement were a collaborative effort between Zarudnyak and Oleg Shynkarenko
. In this performance, the track features the vocals of Nastya Shylo and guitar by Oleh Shynkarenko.
Lyric Analysis
The lyrics present a nostalgic and philosophical reflection on cultural legacy, memory, and the passage of time.
Cultural Eulogy: The song opens by naming iconic progressive and post-punk bands—Van Der Graaf Generator, Siouxsie and the Banshees, and the Grateful Dead—characterizing them as things that have "disappeared" and "are no longer here." This frames the song as a meditation on a lost era of music and art.
Generational Transmission: The imagery of a truck delivering milk to children, who drink it and "remember everything easily," suggests a simplified way in which the next generation consumes information or history, contrasted with the "English words" and complex "layouts" of who was right or wrong.
Intertextual References: The text weaves in significant cultural markers. The phrase "From Nowhere with Love" (Ниоткуда с любовью) is a direct reference to a famous poem by Joseph Brodsky, while "At the last breath" (на последнем дыхании) mirrors the title of Jean-Luc Godard’s seminal French New Wave film. These references elevate the song from a simple indie track to a dense, intellectual collage.
Existential Navigation: The concluding lines focus on the importance of finding a "secret" or a guide through language and memory so that one does not make a mistake in deciding "where to stay and with whom to leave." This suggests that art and culture serve as essential tools for navigating life's moral and physical crossroads.
